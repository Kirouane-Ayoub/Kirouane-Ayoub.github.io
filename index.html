Project 1

Wildfires Detection System

The Wildfire Smoke Detection and Tracking System is a high-tech solution that uses drones or HPWREN cameras equipped with the YOLO algorithm to detect and alert individuals of potential wildfire smoke in the surrounding area. The system is designed to work in real-time, constantly monitoring the environment and providing accurate and timely notifications of any smoke detected. The use of drones allows for a wider coverage area and the ability to access hard-to-reach areas while HPWREN cameras are utilized to monitor remote areas that are at high risk of wildfire. The YOLO algorithm is a powerful machine learning tool that is able to detect and classify objects in real-time, making it a valuable tool in detecting smoke and preventing the spread of wildfire.

Project 3

Rice-Diseases detection System

A Bacterial Leaf Blight and Blast and Brown spot Rice-Diseases detection System is a computer vision system designed to automatically identify and classify three common rice diseases: Bacterial Leaf Blight, Blast, and Brown spot. These diseases can cause significant damage to rice crops, leading to reduced yield and economic losses for farmers. The detection system typically uses image processing techniques and machine learning algorithms to analyze images of rice leaves and identify signs of disease. In the case of Bacterial Leaf Blight, the system may look for characteristic yellowing or necrosis of the leaves. For Blast, the system may detect lesions or spots on the leaves or sheaths. For Brown spot, the system may identify small, dark spots on the leaves.

Project 4

Brain Tumor Detection and Segmentation System

A Brain Tumor Detection and Segmentation System is a computerized system designed to identify and classify brain tumors from medical images such as MRI, CT scans, and PET scans. The system uses advanced algorithms and machine learning techniques to analyze the medical images and highlight regions of the brain that contain abnormal tissue growth. The system consists of several key components, including image preprocessing, segmentation, feature extraction, and classification. Image preprocessing is the first step, where the medical images are prepared for analysis by removing noise, enhancing contrast, and standardizing the image size and orientation.

Project 5

Face Recognition and Verification System

In a face recognition system, the extracted features are compared to a database of known faces to identify the person in the image or video. This technology is used in security systems, law enforcement, and other applications where identifying individuals is critical. In a face verification system, the extracted features are compared to a previously enrolled image or video of the same person to verify their identity. This technology is commonly used for access control, such as allowing authorized personnel to enter a restricted area.

A PPE detection system is a computer vision application that uses machine learning algorithms to detect whether workers are wearing appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) in the workplace. PPE is equipment worn to minimize exposure to various hazards, such as physical, chemical, and biological risks that can cause harm to a worker's health and safety. A PPE detection system can assist in ensuring workplace safety and compliance with occupational health and safety regulations.

Project 7

Bone-fracture Detection System

This system utilizes deep learning algorithms to detect bone fractures in X-ray images. The YOLO model is trained on a labeled dataset of X-ray images with annotated bounding boxes around the fractures. When a new X-ray image is fed into the system, the YOLO model analyzes the image and predicts the location of any fractures present in the image. This information can be displayed to a radiologist or other medical professional to assist in diagnosing the fracture accurately and quickly.
